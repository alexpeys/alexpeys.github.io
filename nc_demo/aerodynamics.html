Recommended Aero Package Configurations

Budget Package (~$1,000)

  • Front: DIY front air dam and splitter ($200-300)
  • Side: Stock or DIY side skirt extensions ($100-200)
  • Rear: Small lip spoiler ($200-300)
  • Underbody: DIY flat bottom panels in critical areas ($200-300)
  • Cooling: Basic brake cooling ducts ($150-200)

Performance Impact: Modest downforce increase, reduced lift, improved stability at speed, basic brake cooling for endurance reliability.

Mid-Range Package (~$2,500)

  • Front: Nine Lives Racing or Racebred Components splitter ($500-700)
  • Side: CarbonMiata or Street Aero side skirts ($300-400)
  • Rear: Nine Lives Racing wing ($1,000-1,200)
  • Underbody: Partial flat bottom with CarbonMiata rear diffuser ($450-650)
  • Cooling: Complete brake cooling kit with 2.5" ducts ($250-300)

Performance Impact: Significant downforce increase (200-300%), balanced front-to-rear, reduced drag, comprehensive brake cooling for endurance racing.

Competition Package (~$4,000+)

  • Front: Complete race splitter with air dam and canards ($800-1,000)
  • Side: Full race-spec side skirts with underbody integration ($500-700)
  • Rear: Adjustable GT wing with custom mounts ($1,200-1,500)
  • Underbody: Complete flat bottom with integrated diffuser ($800-1,200)
  • Cooling: Comprehensive cooling for brakes and engine with custom ducting ($500-700)
  • Additional: Custom vortex generators, gurney flaps, and other detail elements ($200-300)

Performance Impact: Maximum downforce (300-400% increase), optimized for specific tracks, comprehensive thermal management, significant handling improvements at all speeds.

Compliance with Endurance Racing Regulations

Most endurance racing series like Lucky Dog Racing League have relatively open aerodynamic rules compared to sprint racing classes. However, there are some general considerations:

  1. Splitter Protrusion: Most series limit front splitter extension to 2-3 inches beyond the bodywork
  2. Wing Height/Width: Wings typically cannot extend beyond the widest point of the body or above the roofline
  3. Safety Considerations: All aero components must be securely mounted with appropriate hardware
  4. Sharp Edges: No exposed sharp edges that could injure corner workers or other drivers

Always check the specific rulebook for your intended racing series before making significant aerodynamic modifications.

For endurance racing specifically, the balance between downforce and drag is critical. While maximum downforce might yield faster lap times in short sprints, the increased drag can hurt fuel efficiency and top speed on long straights. A well-balanced package that prioritizes stability, cooling, and efficiency will typically outperform a maximum downforce setup over the course of an 8-hour race.
