Fuel System Requirements and Options for NC Miata Endurance Racing

1. Fuel Capacity Analysis and Requirements

Stock System Limitations

  • Stock Tank Capacity: Approximately 12 gallons (45.4 liters)
  • Endurance Racing Challenges:
    • Stock capacity insufficient for 2-hour stints in competitive racing
    • Stock fuel filler pipe restricts flow, causing "burping" with fast-flow fuel cans
    • Stock fuel gauge shows empty with 2-2.5 gallons remaining

Fuel Consumption Estimates

  • Typical Consumption Rate: 6-7 gallons per hour (based on forum data)
  • 2-Hour Stint Requirement: 12-15 gallons minimum capacity
  • Factors Affecting Consumption:
    • Driving style and race pace
    • Track configuration (more corners = higher consumption)
    • Ambient temperature
    • Engine modifications

Installation Considerations

  • Location: Stock tank location preferred for weight distribution
  • Fabrication Required:
    • Modifications to roll cage and firewall often necessary
    • Custom mounting brackets needed
  • Venting Requirements:
    • Even -10 AN venting may be insufficient for quick refueling
    • Consider dual vent lines for optimal performance
  • Certification Expiration:
    • FIA fuel cells have 5-year certification period
    • Must be recertified or replaced after expiration

Fuel Delivery System Considerations

  • Pressure Regulation:
    • Stock regulator adequate for mild performance builds
    • Adjustable regulator recommended for higher-flow pumps
    • Return-style system preferred for consistent fuel delivery
  • Filtration:
    • High-flow fuel filter essential for endurance reliability
    • Consider pre-pump and post-pump filtration
  • Surge Protection:
    • Baffled tank or surge tank recommended to prevent fuel starvation in corners
    • Critical for endurance racing reliability

4. Fuel System Reliability Enhancements

Common Failure Points and Solutions

Component Failure Risk Prevention Measures
Fuel Pump Medium Preventative replacement before races, redundant pumps
Fuel Lines Low High-quality AN fittings, secure mounting away from heat sources
Fuel Filter Medium Regular replacement, monitoring pressure drop
Fuel Cell Foam High Regular inspection, replacement if using ethanol-blended fuels
Fuel Pickup High Proper design with anti-slosh baffling, multiple pickup points

Maintenance Schedule

  • Pre-Race:
    • Pressure test entire fuel system
    • Inspect all connections and fittings
    • Verify fuel pump operation and pressure
  • Annual:
    • Replace fuel filters
    • Inspect fuel cell foam
    • Check fuel cell certification date
  • Bi-Annual:
    • Consider preventative fuel pump replacement
    • Pressure test fuel cell

5. Refueling Equipment and Procedures

Equipment Options

  • Fuel Jugs:
    • VP Racing 5-gallon jugs: $35-45 each
    • Hunsaker quick-dump cans: $250-350 each
  • Refueling Rigs:
    • Gravity-fed systems: $500-1,000
    • Pressurized systems: $1,500-3,000 (check series regulations)

Pit Stop Strategy

  • Lucky Dog Racing League:
    • Typically requires 7-minute pit stops for fueling and driver changes
    • Full fire suits required for refueling crew
  • ChampCar:
    • 5-minute minimum pit stop when refueling
    • Fire suppression equipment must be present

6. Series-Specific Regulations

ChampCar Endurance Series

  • Stock fuel tanks in stock locations OR approved fuel cells are the only fuel sources allowed
  • Fuel cells limited to 2 gallons more than stock capacity (approximately 14.7 gallons for NC Miata)
  • All fuel components must be on the opposite side of a firewall from the driver

Lucky Dog Racing League

  • More accommodating fuel cell regulations than ChampCar
  • Pyrotect discount available for series participants
  • Mandatory pit stop durations (5-7 minutes) when refueling

7. Recommendations

Budget Option (~$500)

  • Retain stock fuel tank
  • Improve venting of stock tank for faster refueling
  • Install Walbro 190lph or Deatschwerks DW200 fuel pump
  • Add high-quality fuel filter and pressure gauge

Mid-Range Option (~$2,000)

  • Install 12-15 gallon Pyrotect or Fuel Safe fuel cell
  • Upgrade to Walbro 190lph pump
  • Add proper venting with dual -10 AN lines
  • Install adjustable fuel pressure regulator

Premium Option (~$3,000+)

  • Custom fabricated 15-gallon fuel cell with optimal placement
  • Dual pump system with automatic failover
  • Integrated surge tank to prevent fuel starvation
  • Quick-fill system for minimal pit stop time

8. Conclusion

The fuel system is a critical component for successful endurance racing with an NC Miata. While the stock 12-gallon tank may be sufficient for shorter stints, competitive 2-hour stints will require either a fuel cell upgrade or extremely efficient fuel management strategies. The balance between capacity, weight, reliability, and refueling efficiency must be carefully considered based on team budget and racing goals.

For most teams, the mid-range option with a properly installed 12-15 gallon fuel cell and upgraded pump represents the best balance of performance, reliability, and cost for endurance racing applications.
