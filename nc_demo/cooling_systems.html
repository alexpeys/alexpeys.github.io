Cooling System Options for NC Miata in Endurance Racing

This comprehensive report details cooling system options for the NC Miata (2006-2015) in endurance racing applications, with a focus on 8-hour races with 2-hour driver stints. Each section covers available options with pricing, installation complexity, and effectiveness ratings.

1. Radiator Upgrades

Radiator upgrades are essential for endurance racing an NC Miata, as the stock unit is inadequate for extended high-performance driving.

Key Considerations for Radiator Selection

  1. Core Thickness: Thicker cores (32mm-42mm) provide significantly better cooling than stock (approximately 16mm)
  2. Flow Design: Multi-pass designs (double or triple) offer superior heat dissipation
  3. Fin Density: Higher fin counts improve heat transfer but may collect debris more easily
  4. Mounting Compatibility: Most aftermarket options are direct fit, but some may require minor modifications
  5. Fan Compatibility: Ensure compatibility with stock or upgraded cooling fans

Installation Notes

  • Radiator installation requires draining the cooling system and removing the front bumper
  • Access is limited in the NC, making installation more challenging than previous generations
  • Professional installation recommended for those without mechanical experience
  • Typical installation time: 3-4 hours

Key Considerations for Oil Cooling Selection

  1. Thermostat Integration: Thermostatic control prevents overcooling in cold conditions
  2. Mounting Location: Front-mounted coolers are most effective but vulnerable to damage
  3. Core Size: Larger cores provide better cooling but add weight and complexity
  4. Plumbing: High-quality lines and fittings are essential to prevent leaks
  5. Sensor Ports: Additional ports for temperature monitoring are valuable for endurance racing

Installation Notes

  • Most systems require removing the front bumper for installation
  • Sandwich plate adapters can stress the oil filter housing and may lead to cracking
  • Direct-fit systems like the SakeBomb "Smart Cooler" eliminate the sandwich plate issue
  • Typical installation time: 2-3 hours

  2. Fluid Circulation: Active systems with pumps provide better cooling than passive systems

  1. Material: Steel ducts resist heat deformation better than carbon fiber or aluminum
  2. Duct Size: 2.5" ducts provide good balance of airflow and fitment
  3. Airflow Path: Direct air to both rotors and calipers for optimal cooling
  4. Mounting: Secure mounting to prevent movement during extended racing

Installation Notes

  • Some systems require drilling or modification to the splash shields
  • Routing of ducting hoses should avoid hot components and moving parts
  • Typical installation time: 2-3 hours

7. Recommended Cooling System Layout

Optimal Component Arrangement

  1. Radiator: Front-mounted, with unobstructed airflow
  2. Oil Cooler: Front-mounted, positioned for airflow and protection
  3. Transmission Cooler: Side or front-mounted, away from exhaust heat
  4. Differential Cooler: Rear-mounted with dedicated airflow
  5. Brake Ducts: Direct path from front air intakes to brake components
  6. Hood Vents: Positioned over areas of highest underhood temperature

Airflow Path Optimization

  1. Intake: Maximize front bumper openings for radiator and cooler airflow
  2. Engine Bay: Remove or relocate obstructions in front of radiator
  3. Extraction: Hood vents positioned for maximum heat removal
  4. Underbody: Smooth airflow under car with strategic ducting

9. Conclusion

Effective cooling system management is critical for NC Miata endurance racing success. The stock cooling systems are inadequate for extended high-performance driving, particularly in warm conditions or with modified engines. A comprehensive approach addressing radiator, oil, transmission, differential, and brake cooling will provide the reliability needed for 8-hour races with 2-hour driver stints.

Key recommendations:

  1. Radiator upgrade should be the first priority
  2. Oil cooling is essential for engine longevity
  3. Hood vents dramatically improve overall cooling effectiveness
  4. Comprehensive temperature monitoring prevents catastrophic failures
  5. Consider the complete cooling package rather than individual components

With proper cooling system upgrades, the NC Miata can be transformed into a reliable and competitive endurance racing platform capable of consistent performance throughout extended racing events.
