Optimizing Driver Changes in Endurance Racing

Recommended Equipment

  1. Fixed-Mounted Seat with Inserts
    • Mount the seat for the medium-sized driver in your lineup
    • Tallest driver sits directly in the shell
    • Medium driver uses factory padding
    • Smallest driver uses pour-in seat insert
    • Avoids the reliability concerns of seat sliders while accommodating different driver heights
  2. Quick-Release Steering Wheel
    • Essential for rapid ingress/egress
    • Lifeline USA or NRG Gen 2.5 highly recommended by Miata racers
    • Practice removal/installation until it becomes muscle memory
  3. Surgical Tubing for Harness Management
    • Attach surgical tubing to harness loops and secure to roll cage
    • When driver hits the cam lock to disconnect, harnesses automatically move out of the way
    • Eliminates fumbling with belts during driver changes
  4. Standardized Connectors
    • Use IMSA or NASCAR-style connectors for all communications and accessories
    • Ensure all drivers have compatible equipment
    • Avoid aviation-style connectors that can be difficult during rushed changes
  5. Velcro Attachments for Hydration/Comms
    • Attach water tube and radio connections to helmet with Velcro
    • Allows for quick disconnection during driver changes
    • Crew can easily locate and reconnect for incoming driver
  6. Window Net with Quality Latch Design
    • Choose a window net with a quick-release mechanism
    • Practice operation until it becomes second nature

Recommended Procedures

  1. Defined Roles During Driver Change
    • Assign a dedicated "driver valet" responsible for:
      • Disconnecting the outgoing driver (harness, comms, hydration)
      • Assisting with exit
      • Helping incoming driver get situated
      • Reconnecting all systems
    • Fuel team members should focus only on refueling
    • Tire/brake team members handle only their assigned tasks
  2. Choreographed Change Sequence
    1. Car arrives at pit stall
    2. Driver valet opens door, disconnects harness cam lock
    3. Outgoing driver removes steering wheel, disconnects radio/hydration
    4. Outgoing driver exits while incoming driver prepares
    5. Incoming driver enters, connects radio/hydration
    6. Driver valet secures harness while driver attaches steering wheel
    7. Final check of all connections before release
  3. Practice Sessions
    • Schedule regular practice of driver changes before race day
    • Time each change and work to improve efficiency
    • Practice in full race gear to simulate actual conditions
    • Practice both standard changes and emergency scenarios
  4. Communication Protocol
    • Establish clear verbal commands for each stage of the change
    • Use consistent terminology between all team members
    • Implement a final "all clear" confirmation before car release
  5. Contingency Planning
    • Develop procedures for communication failures
    • Practice emergency extraction scenarios
    • Have backup plans for equipment failures

By implementing these equipment choices and procedures, a Miata endurance racing team can significantly reduce pit stop times while ensuring driver comfort and safety during the critical 2-hour stints. Regular practice of the driver change procedure is ultimately the most important factor in optimizing this aspect of endurance racing.
