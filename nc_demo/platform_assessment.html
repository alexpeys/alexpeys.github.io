NC Miata Platform Analysis for Endurance Racing

Introduction

The NC Miata (2006-2015) has proven to be a capable and increasingly popular platform for endurance racing series like Lucky Dog. This comprehensive analysis examines the platform's strengths, weaknesses, and recommended modifications to create a reliable and competitive endurance race car.

Platform Overview

Model Year Differences

The NC Miata went through three iterations during its production run:

  • NC1 (2006-2008): First generation with initial design and components
  • NC2 (2009-2012): Facelift with restyled front and rear fascias, improved transmission reliability
  • NC3 (2013-2015): Final update with larger grille opening, revised fog lamp design, and new lip spoiler

Recommendation: NC2 or NC3 models are preferred for endurance racing due to improved reliability, particularly in the 6-speed transmission which had issues in the NC1.

Major Vehicle Systems Analysis

Engine & Powertrain

Stock Limitations:

  • Stock engine reliable but underpowered for competitive racing
  • Oil temperatures can stabilize around 260°F under racing conditions
  • NC1 6-speed transmissions have reliability issues

Recommended Upgrades:

  • Target ~185hp for reliability in endurance racing (vs. 200+ hp for sprint racing)
  • Header, full exhaust, and matched cams for power increase
  • ECUtek tuning for no-lift-to-shift functionality
  • Consider 2.5L engine swap (from Mazda3/6) for more torque and reliability
  • Upgrade to NC2/NC3 transmission if using an NC1

Cooling System

Stock Limitations:

  • Inadequate for extended high-performance driving
  • Airflow restrictions from factory components

Recommended Upgrades:

  • Double-pass or triple-pass radiator (Griffin custom fabricated unit recommended)
  • High-performance cooling fan (essential for endurance racing)
  • Remove or relocate components in front of the engine to improve airflow:
    • Battery relocation
    • ECU relocation
    • Airbox modification
    • Power steering reservoir relocation
  • Hood venting to extract heat
  • Consider oil cooler for extended racing

Suspension & Handling

Stock Limitations:

  • Soft suspension not optimized for racing
  • Higher center of gravity than previous generations

Recommended Upgrades:

  • Coilovers (MeisterR, Fox, Penske, or Koni recommended for endurance racing)
  • Upgraded sway bars (front and rear)
  • RX8 front hubs (direct bolt-on, much stronger)
  • RX8 rear hubs if rules allow (requires careful parts matching)
  • RX8 differential mount
  • Performance alignment settings for endurance racing

Braking System

Stock Limitations:

  • Inadequate for extended track use
  • Potential for fade during long stints

Recommended Upgrades:

  • RX8 brakes all around (direct fit)
  • Performance pads (Raybestos ST43 recommended for front)
  • Vented rear rotors to help prevent hub failures
  • Stainless steel brake lines
  • High-temperature brake fluid

Weight Reduction Opportunities

Target: Reduce from stock ~2550 lbs to as close to 2000 lbs as possible

High-Impact Areas:

  • Remove soft top mechanism (~40 lbs)
  • Lightweight racing seats (~40 lbs savings)
  • Remove sound deadening material (~25 lbs)
  • Remove A/C components (~30 lbs)
  • Lightweight battery or relocation (~20 lbs)
  • Remove interior components (~50 lbs)
  • Lightweight wheels (~20 lbs)
  • Carbon fiber or fiberglass body panels (~30 lbs)
  • Lexan windows (~15 lbs)
  • Remove spare tire and jack (~20 lbs)
  • Lightweight exhaust (~15 lbs)

Note: A fully race-prepped NC can get within 100 lbs of an equally prepared NB Miata.

Fuel System

Stock Limitations:

  • Stock fuel pump may fail after extended use
  • Limited fuel capacity for long stints

Recommended Upgrades:

  • Upgraded fuel pump for reliability
  • Consider fuel cell installation for endurance racing (requires careful planning)

Successful Existing Builds

Several successful NC Miata endurance racing builds have demonstrated the platform's potential:

  1. ChampCar NC Builds:
    • Typically running ~167whp with stock engine
    • Custom ram-air intake and header
    • Weight reduction to ~1950 lbs
    • 255 tires on 17x10 wheels
  2. Goodwin Racing NC Light:
    • Extensive weight reduction
    • Target of 300hp and 2000 lbs
    • Serves as test bed for budget racer setup
  3. Lucky Dog Racing NC Miatas:
    • Recently welcomed into the series
    • Competitive with careful preparation
    • Focus on reliability over maximum performance

Conclusion

The NC Miata presents a viable and increasingly popular platform for endurance racing. While it has several known weaknesses, particularly in the rear hubs and cooling system, these can be addressed with targeted upgrades. The platform offers good potential for weight reduction and power increases while maintaining reliability.

For endurance racing specifically, focus on:

  1. Cooling system improvements
  2. Rear hub reinforcement or replacement
  3. Moderate power increases (target ~185hp)
  4. Extensive weight reduction
  5. Upgraded braking system

With these modifications, an NC Miata can be transformed into a competitive and reliable endurance racer capable of completing 8-hour races with 2-hour driver stints as specified for Lucky Dog racing series.
