NC Miata Endurance Race Car: Detailed Build Plan

The executive summary for this plan can be found on the Home Page.

1. Base Vehicle Selection

Recommended: 2009-2015 Mazda MX-5 Miata (NC2/NC3)

  • Budget: $7,000-9,000
  • Target: Manual transmission, moderate mileage (under 100,000 miles)
  • Rationale: NC2/NC3 models (2009+) have improved transmission durability and better overall reliability

Key Pre-Purchase Inspection Points:

  • Compression test (minimum 180 psi across all cylinders)
  • Transmission operation (particularly 3rd gear synchros)
  • Differential operation and noise
  • Rust inspection (particularly frame rails and suspension mounting points)
  • Accident history verification

2. Safety Equipment

Roll Cage

  • Specification: Professional fabricated 8-point cage with NASCAR-style door bars
  • Budget: $2,500-3,000
  • Supplier: Local motorsport fabricator with endurance racing experience
  • Notes: Must meet or exceed Lucky Dog series requirements; include mounting points for harness and seat

Fire Suppression

  • Specification: SFI 17.1 approved system with Novec
  • Budget: $750-900
  • Supplier: Safecraft (Model: PB10L) or SPA Design
  • Notes: Dual nozzle system with automatic and manual triggers

Seats & Restraints

  • Specification: FIA-approved fixed racing seat with side protection
  • Budget: $600-800
  • Supplier: Sparco Circuit II ($699) or OMP HTE-R ($749)
  • Notes: Select seat that accommodates all drivers; consider seat inserts for different driver sizes

Harness

  • Specification: 6-point FIA-approved harness
  • Budget: $350-450
  • Supplier: Schroth Profi II-6 ($399) or OMP First 6-point ($379)
  • Notes: Ensure proper mounting to roll cage and/or chassis

Additional Safety Items

  • Window net: SFI-approved with quick release ($150)
  • Kill switch: Dual circuit with accessible external switch ($150)
  • Tow hooks: High-quality front and rear ($150)
  • Safety wire kit: Comprehensive kit with tools ($100)

3. Cooling System

Radiator

  • Specification: CSF Triple-Pass 42mm radiator
  • Budget: $1,030 (based on Future Motorsports pricing)
  • Supplier: CSF/Good-Win Racing (Part #61-3390)
  • Notes: Requires trimming of some plastics for fitment

Oil Cooling

  • Specification: MiataSpeed FPSpec "Smart Cooler" (Thermostatic)
  • Budget: $695-795
  • Supplier: MiataSpeed
  • Notes: Thermostatic control prevents overcooling during warm-up

Cooling Fans

  • Specification: High-flow electric fan (minimum 2,500 CFM)
  • Budget: $200-300
  • Supplier: SPAL 16" High Performance Fan
  • Notes: Essential for cooling during caution periods

Additional Cooling Components

  • Differential cooling plate: Mazda Motorsports ($200)
  • Hood vents: Custom fabricated or Race Louvers kit ($300-500)
  • High-temperature silicone hoses: Samco Sport ($250-350)
  • High-capacity aluminum radiator cap: Mishimoto ($30)

4. Hub Reinforcement

Front Hubs

  • Specification: RX8 Front Hub Conversion
  • Budget: $250-300 per pair
  • Supplier: Good-Win Racing or Mazda Motorsports
  • Notes: Direct bolt-on upgrade with higher load rating

Rear Hubs

  • Specification: Billet Enduro Rear Hubs
  • Budget: $800-1,000 per pair
  • Supplier: Miata Hubs or Miatacage Motorsports
  • Notes: Critical reliability upgrade; alternative is regular replacement of OEM hubs every 25 hours

Wheel Bearings

  • Specification: Premium quality bearings
  • Budget: $200-300 for all corners
  • Supplier: Timken
  • Notes: Replace all bearings during hub upgrades

5. Suspension & Handling

Coilovers

  • Specification: MeisterR ClubRace 10k/6k
  • Budget: $1,395
  • Supplier: Good-Win Racing
  • Notes: Good balance of performance and reliability for endurance racing

Sway Bars

  • Specification: Flyin' Miata Front/Rear Adjustable
  • Budget: $399
  • Supplier: Flyin' Miata
  • Notes: Adjustable to fine-tune handling balance

Bushings

  • Specification: SuperPro Enhancement Kit
  • Budget: $699
  • Supplier: SuperPro
  • Notes: Polyurethane for improved precision without excessive harshness

Alignment Specifications

  • Front Camber: -2.5° to -3.0°
  • Rear Camber: -2.0° to -2.5°
  • Front Toe: 0 to 1/16" total toe-out
  • Rear Toe: 1/16" to 1/8" total toe-in
  • Front Caster: Maximum positive

6. Brakes

Calipers

  • Specification: Rebuilt stock with high-temp seals
  • Budget: $200-300
  • Supplier: Local rebuilder or DIY
  • Notes: Stock calipers are adequate when properly maintained

Pads

  • Specification: PFC 01 front, PFC 04 rear
  • Budget: $350-450
  • Supplier: PFC or authorized dealer
  • Notes: Balance of initial bite, fade resistance, and wear rate

Rotors

  • Specification: Slotted rotors with ARP extended studs
  • Budget: $350-450
  • Supplier: StopTech or Centric
  • Notes: Avoid drilled rotors for endurance racing

Brake Lines

  • Specification: Stainless steel braided lines
  • Budget: $150-200
  • Supplier: Goodridge or Russell
  • Notes: Improves pedal feel and consistency

Brake Fluid

  • Specification: Motul RBF 660 or similar
  • Budget: $100-150
  • Supplier: Motul
  • Notes: Minimum 600°F dry boiling point

Brake Cooling

  • Specification: AWR Racing Brake Cooling Duct Kit
  • Budget: $455
  • Supplier: AWR Racing
  • Notes: Essential for endurance racing to prevent brake fade

7. Drivetrain Reliability

Clutch

  • Specification: ACT XT/Race Sprung 6 Pad
  • Budget: $800-900
  • Supplier: ACT
  • Notes: Balance of durability and drivability

Differential

  • Specification: OEM Torsen rebuild with new bearings
  • Budget: $700-800
  • Supplier: Local specialist or DIY
  • Notes: Stock Torsen is adequate when properly maintained

Transmission

  • Specification: NC2/NC3 transmission (if not already equipped)
  • Budget: $1,000-1,200
  • Supplier: Used from donor car or Mazda Motorsports
  • Notes: Stronger 3rd gear synchro hub than NC1

Engine Reliability Upgrades

  • Valve springs: MazdaSpeed 3 upgrade ($300-400)
  • Oil pan baffle: Moroso or custom fabricated ($200-300)
  • ARP head studs: ($250-350)
  • MLS head gasket: Cometic ($150-200)

8. Fuel System

Fuel Cell

  • Specification: 12-15 gallon SFI-approved cell
  • Budget: $1,000-1,200
  • Supplier: Fuel Safe or ATL
  • Notes: Requires custom mounting and plumbing

Fuel Pump

  • Specification: Walbro 255lph
  • Budget: $180-220
  • Supplier: Walbro
  • Notes: Reliable upgrade over stock pump

Fuel Lines

  • Specification: -8AN lines with high-quality fittings
  • Budget: $300-400
  • Supplier: Earl's or Aeromotive
  • Notes: Use PTFE-lined hose for ethanol compatibility

  • Budget: $400-500
  • Supplier: Sparco or OMP
  • Notes: Facilitates driver changes and entry/exit

Gauges/Data

  • Specification: AiM Solo 2 DL data logger
  • Budget: $700-800
  • Supplier: AiM Sports
  • Notes: Provides critical data for driver feedback and car setup

Pedals

  • Specification: Floor-mounted pedal assembly
  • Budget: $250-350
  • Supplier: Wilwood or OBP
  • Notes: Improves ergonomics for different driver sizes

Communications

  • Specification: Quality radio with helmet connections
  • Budget: $250-350
  • Supplier: Racing Radios or similar
  • Notes: Essential for team communication during races

10. Aerodynamics

Front Splitter

  • Specification: Nine Lives Racing or Racebred Components
  • Budget: $600-700
  • Supplier: Nine Lives Racing
  • Notes: Moderate downforce without excessive drag

Side Skirts

Reliability Considerations and Preventative Measures

Critical Failure Points and Mitigation Strategies

1. Cooling System

Failure Risk: ⚠️⚠️⚠️⚠️⚠️ (Extremely High)
Mitigation:

  • Triple-pass radiator with maximum cooling capacity
  • High-flow electric fan with manual override
  • Thermostatic oil cooler to prevent overcooling
  • Hood vents for heat extraction
  • Regular inspection of hoses and connections
  • Temperature monitoring during races
2. Rear Hubs

Failure Risk: ⚠️⚠️⚠️⚠️⚠️ (Extremely High)
Mitigation:

  • Billet enduro rear hubs or regular replacement schedule
  • Regular inspection for cracks or play
  • Temperature monitoring during races
  • Carry spare hubs in pit
3. Front Hubs

Failure Risk: ⚠️⚠️⚠️⚠️ (Very High)
Mitigation:

  • RX8 front hub conversion
  • Regular inspection of bearings
  • ARP wheel studs
4. Engine (when pushed)

Failure Risk: ⚠️⚠️⚠️⚠️ (Very High)
Mitigation:

  • Conservative power target (~185HP)
  • Upgraded valve springs
  • Oil pan baffle to prevent oil starvation
  • Regular oil analysis
  • Comprehensive cooling system
5. Transmission

Failure Risk: ⚠️⚠️⚠️ (High)
Mitigation:

  • Use NC2/NC3 transmission
  • Careful shifting technique
  • Regular fluid changes
  • Avoid "no-lift shift" features

Testing and Shakedown Recommendations

Initial Shakedown (Parking Lot)

  • Low-speed systems check, Brake system function, Steering response, Electrical systems, Fluid leaks

Drivetrain Test Day

  • Progressive load testing, Shift quality assessment, Clutch engagement, Differential operation, Temperature monitoring

Cooling System Test

  • Extended running at race pace, Caution period simulation (low speed/idle), Temperature monitoring, Fan operation verification, Oil temperature stabilization

Suspension & Brake Test Day

  • Balance assessment, Brake modulation and feel, Tire wear patterns, Handling consistency over multiple laps, Driver feedback collection

Full Race Simulation

  • 2-hour continuous running, Driver changes if possible, Full systems monitoring, Fuel consumption measurement, Tire wear assessment

Appendix D: Supporting Research

This build plan is based on extensive research from experienced NC Miata endurance racers, with particular attention to reliability concerns and proven solutions. Key insights include:

  1. Cooling system inadequacies are the primary cause of DNFs
  2. Rear hub failures are catastrophic and common
  3. Conservative power targets (185HP vs. 210+HP) significantly improve engine reliability
  4. NC2/NC3 transmissions have improved durability over NC1
  5. Regular preventative maintenance is essential for endurance success

Conclusion

This build plan provides a comprehensive approach to creating a reliable and competitive NC Miata for endurance racing. By focusing on critical reliability upgrades and a systematic build process, the resulting race car will be capable of completing 8-hour endurance races with minimal mechanical issues.

The mid-range budget approach balances performance and reliability, with strategic investments in cooling, hubs, and safety equipment. Following the detailed preventative maintenance schedule will further enhance reliability and race finishing potential.

With proper preparation and execution of this build plan, the NC Miata will be a competitive and enjoyable platform for endurance racing, capable of consistent finishes and strong results in series like Lucky Dog.
