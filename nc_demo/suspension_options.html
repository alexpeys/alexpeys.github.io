Key Considerations for Endurance Racing

  1. Reliability Over Maximum Performance: For endurance racing, prioritize components that can withstand 8+ hours of continuous use over those that provide maximum performance for short durations.
  2. Heat Management: Ensure suspension components have adequate heat dissipation capabilities, especially for long stints.
  3. Serviceability: Choose components that can be quickly adjusted or replaced during pit stops if necessary.
  4. Tire Wear: Alignment settings and suspension tuning should focus on consistent performance and even tire wear rather than maximum grip.
  5. Driver Comfort: Suspension should be set up to minimize driver fatigue over long stints, which may require slightly softer settings than sprint racing.
  6. Adjustability: Components with multiple adjustment points allow for fine-tuning based on specific track conditions and driver preferences.
  7. Weight: Lighter components reduce unsprung weight, improving handling and reducing stress on other components.
  8. Budget Allocation: If working with a limited budget, prioritize reliability upgrades (like RX8 front hubs) over performance enhancements.
