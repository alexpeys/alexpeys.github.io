Quantitative Analysis of Clover's Potential Price Impact

This analysis estimates Clover's potential impact on consumer prices based on its market position, fee structure, and the economics of payment processing.

Assumptions

  • Clover's annualized payment volume (Q1 2024): $274 billion
  • Estimated 2024 US Personal Consumption Expenditures (PCE): $19.84 trillion (based on average of Q1 & Q4 2024 FRED data)
  • Average transaction size: $50
  • Transaction mix: 80% in-person, 20% online/keyed-in
  • Average in-person fee: 2.5% (weighted average of 2.3%, 2.5%, 2.6% rates, approximated)
  • Online fee: 3.5% + 10¢
  • Assumption: Processing fees are fully passed through to consumers.

Estimate of Total Annual Fees Collected by Clover

Calculating effective percentage rates including the fixed fee:

  • In-person effective rate: ~2.5% (assuming 2.3% + 10¢ = 2.3% + 0.2% = 2.5% on $50 avg)
  • Online/keyed-in effective rate: 3.7% (3.5% + 10¢ = 3.5% + 0.2% = 3.7% on $50 avg)

Weighted average effective fee rate:

(2.5% * 0.80) + (3.7% * 0.20) = 2.0% + 0.74% = 2.74%

Estimated total annual fees collected by Clover (based on payment volume):

$274 billion * 2.74% = ~$7.51 billion

Note: This estimate is based on processing fees alone and may differ from Clover's reported revenue ($2.1 billion in 2023), which includes hardware sales, software subscriptions, etc. The fee calculation likely overestimates actual fee revenue captured directly by Clover/Fiserv as interchange fees pass through to issuing banks. However, it represents the total cost imposed on merchants (and potentially consumers) through the Clover system's processing function.

Fees as Percentage of Total US Consumer Spending

Estimated Clover processing fees / Estimated 2024 US PCE:

$7.51 billion / $19,840 billion = ~0.038%

This suggests that the total fees processed specifically through Clover represent a very small fraction (less than 0.04%) of overall US consumer spending.

Comparison of Clover's Fee Structure to Industry Averages

As noted in the Clover Profile:

  • Clover's in-person rates (2.3%-2.6% + 10¢) can be competitive, potentially lower than Square's standard rates (2.5%-2.6% + 10¢), especially for restaurants (2.3% + 10¢).
  • Clover's online rate (3.5% + 10¢) is higher than Square's (2.9% + 30¢ - Note: Square's online fee structure varies).
  • Clover allows merchants to use their own merchant accounts, which can lead to lower negotiated rates for high-volume businesses compared to aggregators like Square, aligning it more with traditional merchant service providers.
  • Overall, Clover's advertised rates are within the typical range for the industry, particularly for integrated POS solutions targeting SMBs.

Sector Penetration and Inflation Rates

Clover has a strong presence in:

  • Restaurants
  • Retail
  • Professional Services

Comparing inflation in these sectors (using data from the US Inflation Analysis task, focusing on recent trends where services dominate):

  • Restaurants (Food Away From Home): Historically showed significant inflation post-pandemic, often exceeding general CPI, driven by food costs, labor, and rent. Menu prices rose 27.2% Feb 2020 - June 2024 (NRA data).
  • Retail (Goods): Goods inflation was high initially but moderated significantly or turned negative more recently, contrasting with Clover's growth period.
  • Services (General): Services inflation, particularly core services ex-housing, has been persistent and a major driver of recent overall inflation. Professional services fall into this broader category.

Analysis: While restaurants, a key Clover sector, experienced high inflation, this is widely attributed to input costs (food, labor) rather than specifically payment processing. The divergence between high services inflation (where Clover is expanding) and lower goods inflation (a traditional Clover base) doesn't provide a clear signal linking Clover penetration directly to higher sectoral inflation. Many other factors influence sector-specific inflation.

Estimated Potential Price Impact (Full Pass-Through)

If Clover's estimated total processing fees ($7.51 billion) were fully passed through to consumers uniformly across the $274 billion volume processed, it would represent an average price increase of 2.74% *on those specific transactions processed by Clover*.

However, spreading this cost across the entire US consumer spending ($19.84 trillion) results in a negligible direct impact on the overall inflation rate (estimated contribution of ~0.038 percentage points, assuming uniform distribution, which is unrealistic).

The more relevant impact is the potential 2-4% cost added to transactions processed by Clover merchants, which those merchants might incorporate into their overall pricing structure. This contributes to the broader impact of payment processing costs identified in the literature review but doesn't single out Clover as having a uniquely significant inflationary effect compared to the industry average.

Summary of Quantitative Analysis

Based on Clover's reported payment volume and standard fee structure, the total annual processing fees facilitated through its system are estimated at ~$7.51 billion. While this represents a potential average cost increase of ~2.74% on transactions processed via Clover (assuming full pass-through), these fees constitute a very small fraction (~0.038%) of total US consumer spending. Clover's fee structure is comparable to industry competitors. Analysis of inflation in sectors with high Clover penetration does not show a clear pattern directly attributable to Clover, as sector-specific factors (like food and labor costs in restaurants) dominate. Therefore, while Clover participates in the general mechanism where processing fees add costs for merchants (and potentially consumers), this analysis does not support the claim that Clover *specifically* has a *significant* and *disproportionate* impact on overall US inflation compared to other processors or major economic factors.
