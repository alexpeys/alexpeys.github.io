Company Background

History and Founding

  • Founded in October 2010 by Leonard Speiser, John Beatty, and Kelvin Zheng
  • Initially raised $5.5 million in November 2010, with lead investor Sutter Hill Ventures and participation from Andreessen Horowitz
  • Secured an additional $3 million convertible note from Sutter Hill Ventures in April 2012
  • Launched as a cloud-based Android point of sale (POS) platform in April 2012
  • Acquired by First Data Corporation on December 28, 2012
  • First Data was subsequently acquired by Fiserv on July 29, 2019

Ownership and Corporate Structure

  • Currently owned by Fiserv, Inc. (NYSE: FI), a leading global provider of payments and financial technology solutions
  • Operates as a subsidiary within Fiserv's merchant solutions business segment
  • Headquartered in Sunnyvale, California, United States

Business Model

  • Cloud-based Android point of sale (POS) platform for small and medium-sized businesses
  • Offers integrated payment processing, hardware, and software solutions
  • Revenue streams include:
    • Hardware sales (POS terminals, card readers, etc.)
    • Software subscription fees (monthly plans)
    • Payment processing fees (percentage + fixed fee per transaction)
    • Value-added services (e.g., inventory management, employee scheduling)
  • Open architecture platform allowing third-party app integration through the Clover App Market

Market Position

Market Share and Growth

  • Clover generated $2.1 billion in revenue in 2023, with 30% growth in Q4 2023
  • Fiserv expects Clover to double revenue over the next two years:
    • Projected to reach $3.5 billion in 2025
    • Projected to reach $4.5 billion in 2026
  • As of Q1 2024, Clover's payment volume reached an annualized $274 billion, a 19% increase year-over-year
  • Revenue increased by 30% in Q1 2024 compared to the same period in 2023

Competitive Landscape

  • Clover's $272 billion in annualized sales volume (as of late 2023) exceeds that of Square and Toast
  • Major competitors include: Square (Block, Inc.), Toast, Shift4, Shopify, Lightspeed Commerce, NCR, Oracle
  • Clover differentiates itself with an Android-based open platform approach

Growth Strategy

  • Expanding into new verticals (e.g., professional services)
  • International expansion
  • Launching new Clover products (hardware and software)
  • Deepening penetration in existing markets (e.g., restaurants)

Transaction Volumes and Economic Footprint

Payment Processing Volume

  • As of Q1 2024: $274 billion in annualized payment volume (19% YoY growth)
  • As of Q4 2022: $201 billion in annualized payment volume (50% YoY growth)
  • As of Q3 2020: $133 billion in annualized card transactions worldwide

Market Impact

  • Largest U.S. cloud POS firm as of Q3 2020
  • Significant driver of revenue growth for Fiserv
  • Fiserv's Merchant solutions unit reported $2.25 billion in Q1 2024 revenue (up 13% YoY)
  • Small businesses accounted for two-thirds of this revenue, growing at a 16% rate

Merchant Adoption

Target Markets

  • Primary focus on small and medium-sized businesses (SMBs)
  • Strong presence in: Restaurants, Retail, Professional services, Home and field services, Personal services

Device Deployment

  • Clover Go: Mobile card reader ($199)
  • Clover Flex: Mobile POS with receipt printer ($749)
  • Clover Mini: Compact countertop POS ($849)
  • Clover Station Solo: Full-size POS system ($1,799)
  • Clover Station Duo: Two-screen POS system ($1,899)
  • Self-ordering kiosk ($3,499 + $34.95/month)

Distribution Channels

  • Sold through various financial institutions and payment processors
  • Bank of America Merchant Services was the first announced distributor (Oct 2013)
  • PNC Merchant Services was the second
  • Now available through numerous banks and payment service providers

Fee Structure and Pricing

Software Subscription Fees

Monthly POS software costs range from $0 to $129.85 depending on the plan.

  • $0 for basic plans
  • $14.95 for mid-tier plans
  • $59.95-$84.95 for standard plans
  • $89.95-$129.85 for advanced plans

Transaction Processing Fees

  • In-person transactions:
    • 2.3% + 10¢ (Restaurant plans)
    • 2.5% + 10¢ (Retail/Services Standard/Advanced)
    • 2.6% + 10¢ (Retail/Services Starter)
  • Online or keyed-in transactions: 3.5% + 10¢
  • Custom rates may be available for higher volume businesses.

Hardware Costs

One-time costs for POS hardware range from $199 (Clover Go) to $3,499 (Kiosk).

Competitive Comparison (vs. Square)

  • Clover may offer lower in-person rates (2.3% vs. 2.5-2.6%).
  • Clover's online rates are higher (3.5% vs. 2.9%).
  • Clover hardware is generally more expensive.
  • Clover allows own merchant account setup (potential savings for larger businesses).

Business Evolution and Market Impact

Key Business Changes

  • Acquisition by First Data (2012)
  • Integration into Fiserv (2019)
  • Expansion from payment processing to business management platform
  • Continuous hardware/software development
  • Growing international focus

Economic Significance

  • Major player in the $5 trillion cloud POS market
  • Significant contributor to Fiserv's revenue
  • Facilitates hundreds of billions in annual payment volume
  • Critical infrastructure for SMBs
  • Enables digital transformation

Summary

Clover, owned by Fiserv, is a leading US payment network and POS provider with over $274 billion in annualized payment volume and rapid revenue growth. Its open platform, diverse hardware, and industry-specific solutions drive adoption, particularly in restaurants and retail. Clover's economic footprint is expanding, with revenue projected to hit $4.5 billion by 2026. Its fee structure is competitive, especially for higher-volume businesses. Clover's influence on the US payment landscape is expected to grow.
