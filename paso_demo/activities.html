Dog-Friendly Activities in Paso Robles

Always bring water, waste bags, and proper identification for your dog. Keep dogs leashed unless in designated off-leash areas. Check ahead with specific venues as pet policies may change.

Parks and Outdoor Spaces

Sherwood Dog Park

  • Description: 1.25-acre off-leash dog park with separate areas for big and small dogs, water stations, dog bags, and a dunking pool.
  • Location: Sherwood Field (historic site of Paso Robles' first airport), near 240 Scott St, Paso Robles.
  • Hours: Open daily.
  • Fees: $1 day-use fee or $30 annual membership (check current status).
  • Amenities: Water stations, shade structures, park benches, picnic tables, recycled wood chip ground cover.

Barney Schwartz Park

  • Description: Beautiful park with walking paths around a lake, dog-friendly (on leash).
  • Location: 2970 Union Road, Paso Robles
  • Hours: Open daily from dawn to dusk.
  • Fees: Free.
  • Amenities: Lake, picnic areas, walking paths.
  • Contact: (805) 237-3988 (Paso Robles Recreation Services)

Larry Moore Park

  • Description: Park with beautiful walking trail along Paso Robles Creek; access point for Salinas River Walk.
  • Location: 155 Riverbank Lane, Paso Robles
  • Hours: Open daily from dawn to dusk.
  • Fees: Free.
  • Amenities: Walking trails, river views.
  • Contact: (805) 237-3988 (Paso Robles Recreation Services)

Walking Trails and Hiking Options

Salinas River Walk

  • Description: Scenic 2-mile paved and wheelchair accessible path along the Salinas River.
  • Location: Access from Larry Moore Park on the river's east side, or from various points.
  • Length: Approximately 2 miles (part of planned 30-mile river trail).
  • Difficulty: Easy.
  • Hours: Open daily from dawn to dusk.
  • Fees: Free.
  • Note: Dogs must be kept on leash.
  • Contact: (805) 237-3988 (Paso Robles Recreation Services)

Centennial Trail

  • Description: Easy-access trail extending about a mile along the 16-acre Centennial Park.
  • Location: Centennial Park, 600 Nickerson Dr, Paso Robles
  • Length: Approximately 1 mile.
  • Difficulty: Easy.
  • Hours: Open daily from dawn to dusk.
  • Fees: Free.
  • Note: Dogs must be kept on leash.
  • Contact: (805) 237-3988 (Paso Robles Recreation Services)

Oak Knoll and Quail Roost Trail

  • Description: Quiet, less-trafficked trail less than a mile long.
  • Location: Near end of Fontana Rd, Paso Robles (check local maps for precise access).
  • Length: Less than 1 mile.
  • Difficulty: Easy.
  • Hours: Open year-round.
  • Fees: Free.
  • Note: Dogs must be kept on leash.

Rambouillet Snead Trail

  • Description: Easy one-mile trail, perfect for older dogs.
  • Location: Off Rambouillet Rd, Paso Robles (check local trail maps).
  • Length: 1 mile.
  • Difficulty: Easy.
  • Hours: Open year-round.
  • Fees: Free.
  • Note: Dogs must be kept on leash.

Lake Nacimiento Shoreline Trail

  • Description: Trail wrapping around parts of Lake Nacimiento, good for energetic dogs. Lake Nacimiento Resort has day use fees.
  • Location: Lake Nacimiento, near Paso Robles (approx. 30 min drive). Check resort for trail access.
  • Length: Varies, up to 5.9 miles for some sections.
  • Difficulty: Moderate.
  • Hours: Open year-round (check resort hours/access).
  • Fees: Park entrance/day use fees may apply.
  • Note: Dogs must be kept on leash.

Montebello Oaks Open Space Trail

  • Description: Dog-friendly 2.1-mile lightly trafficked out and back trail.
  • Location: End of Salida Del Sol, Paso Robles (confirm access).
  • Length: 2.1 miles.
  • Difficulty: Easy to moderate.
  • Hours: Open daily from dawn to dusk.
  • Fees: Free.
  • Note: Dogs must be kept on leash.

Dog-Friendly Shops and Markets

Paso Market Walk

  • Description: Mixed-use development with shops, restaurants, and merchants. Outdoor areas are typically dog-friendly.
  • Location: 1803 Spring Street, Paso Robles
  • Shops/Restaurants include: Just Baked, The Vreamery, Momotaro Ramen, Paso Robles Wine Merchant, and more.
  • Hours: Vary by merchant (generally open daily).
  • Note: Confirm with individual merchants about dog policies inside their establishments. Outdoor common areas are generally welcoming.

Downtown Paso Robles

  • Description: Many shops in downtown Paso Robles welcome well-behaved, leashed dogs. Look for "dog-friendly" signs or ask before entering.
  • Location: Centered around Downtown City Park, Spring Street, Park Street.
  • Hours: Vary by business.

Indoor/Covered Options for Inclement Weather (Confirm Dog Policies)

While many activities are outdoors, here are some ideas if weather is an issue. Always confirm specific dog policies for indoor access.

  • Dog-Friendly Restaurants with Covered Patios:
    • Thomas Hill Organics (covered, heated patio) - 1313 Park Street, (805) 226-5888
    • Fish Gaucho (covered patio area) - 1244 Park Street, (805) 239-3333
    • The Hatch Rotisserie & Bar (covered outdoor area) - 835 13th Street, (805) 221-5727
    • Some areas of Paso Market Walk are covered.
  • Dog-Friendly Wineries with Indoor/Covered Tasting Areas:
    • Eberle Winery (dogs allowed on cave tours) - 3810 E Highway 46, (805) 238-9607
    • BarrelHouse Brewing Co. (large covered patio/beer garden) - 3055 Limestone Way, (805) 296-1128
    • Many wineries in Tin City have roll-up doors offering an indoor/outdoor feel. Call specific wineries like Turtle Rock or ONX to ask about their setup.
